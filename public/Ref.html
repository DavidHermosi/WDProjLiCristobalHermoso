References

  • Korean Martial Arts where Tae means "foot", Kwon means "fist", and Do means "art or the way".
  • Taekwondo also means to use all body parts the correct way to stop fights, and bring peace.
  • Around 80 million people do this sport in Asia, Africa, Pan America, and Oceana.
  • Taekwondo originated from Korea and was based on earlier self-defense, Tae Kyon and Karate. Choi Hong-Hi, the principal of Taekwondo, founded this martial art in 1955.

Notable Techniques

Tornado Kick (회오리 차기)

▼

The Tornado Kick is a spectacular spinning kick that combines a 360-degree rotation with a powerful roundhouse kick. This advanced technique showcases the dynamic kicking abilities that Taekwondo is famous for.

540 Kick (540도 차기)

▼

The 540 Kick is one of the most impressive aerial techniques in Taekwondo. The practitioner spins 540 degrees in the air before delivering a powerful kick, demonstrating extraordinary athleticism and control.

Axe Kick (내려 차기)

▼

The Axe Kick involves raising the leg high and bringing the heel down in a chopping motion. This powerful vertical strike is effective for breaking through an opponent's guard and demonstrates flexibility and precision.
