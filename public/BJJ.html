Karate (Á©∫Êâã)

  • Karate means "empty hand" and is a striking martial art that originated in Okinawa, Japan.
  • It emphasizes powerful punches, kicks, knee strikes, and open-hand techniques.
  • Karate is built on three pillars: kata (forms), kumite (sparring), and kihon (basics).
  • The art develops both physical prowess and mental discipline through rigorous training.

  • Black Belt (1st Dan and beyond) Master level - continuous improvement and deep understanding

Zenkutsu Dachi (Front Stance)

‚ñº

Zenkutsu Dachi is the most fundamental offensive stance in Karate. The front leg is bent with 60% of weight forward, while the back leg is straight. This stance provides strong forward momentum for powerful punching and striking techniques.

Kokutsu Dachi (Back Stance)

‚ñº

Kokutsu Dachi is a defensive stance with 70% of weight on the back leg. The back leg is bent while the front leg extends forward. This stance allows for quick evasion and powerful counter-attacks while maintaining defensive positioning.

Kiba Dachi (Horse Stance)

‚ñº

Kiba Dachi is a wide, stable stance resembling a person riding a horse. Both legs are equally bent with feet parallel. This stance builds leg strength and provides a strong base for side strikes and multiple directional attacks.

Mae Geri (Front Kick)

‚ñº

Mae Geri is the fundamental front snap kick in Karate. The kick is delivered straight forward using the ball of the foot, targeting the opponent's midsection or face. It emphasizes speed, accuracy, and the snap-back motion.

Mawashi Geri (Roundhouse Kick)

‚ñº

Mawashi Geri is a circular kick that travels in an arc toward the target. Using the instep or ball of the foot, this kick can target various heights from low leg to head level. It's one of the most versatile and powerful kicks in Karate.

Ushiro Geri (Back Kick)

‚ñº

Ushiro Geri is a powerful back kick delivered with the heel. The practitioner pivots and drives the heel straight back into the target. This technique generates tremendous power and is effective for creating distance from an opponent.
