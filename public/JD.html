Shizen-Hontai (Basic Natural Posture)

‚ñº

The fundamental stance where feet are about shoulder-width apart, knees slightly bent, back straight, and weight evenly distributed.

Jigo-Hontai (Basic Defensive Stance)

‚ñº

Jigo-Hontai is a fundamental judo stance where a judoka lowers their center of gravity and widens their feet for maximum stability to resist an opponent's throws or off-balancing attempts.

Daeshi Harai (Forward Foot Sweep)

‚ñº

A fundamental sweeping technique that targets the opponent's advanced foot just as it is about to bear weight. The thrower (tori) uses a coordinated action of sweeping the foot out while pulling the opponent off balance.

Sasae Tsurikomi Ashi (Lifting Pull Ankle Drop)

‚ñº

This technique is often taught early as it emphasizes the concept of tsurikomi (lifting and pulling). The tori places the sole of their foot against the opponent's ankle to block it, while simultaneously using a lifting and pulling action with the hands to rotate the opponent over the obstructing foot.

Ouchi Gari (Major Inner Reaping)

‚ñº

A major inner leg sweep/reap. The tori steps inside their opponent's stance and uses the back of their leg to sweep the inside of the opponent's supporting leg while driving the opponent backward.

O Soto Gari (Major Outer Reaping)

‚ñº

A major outer leg sweep/reap, often considered one of the most iconic judo throws. The tori circles around the opponent and uses their own leg to forcefully sweep the outside of the opponent's leg, driving them directly backward onto the mat.

Uchi Mata (Inner Thigh Throw)

‚ñº

A dynamic and powerful throw that is a favorite in high-level competition. The tori drives their leg high up between the opponent's thighs and uses a powerful upward and rotational motion with the leg and hips to lift and project the opponent over their hip.
