Stances (ÏÑúÍ∏∞)

Charyeot Seogi (Attention Stance)

‚ñº

Charyeot Seogi is a formal stance used to show respect and readiness. The feet are together, and the heels touching. The body is upright with hands at the sides, symbolizing discipline and focus.

Junbi Seogi (Ready Stance)

‚ñº

Junbi Seogi is one of the common ready positions in Taekwondo. It is usually used to prepare for movement or techniques. The feet are shoulder-width apart, knees slightly bent, and hands are positioned in front of the body, ready for action.

Ap Kubi Seogi (Forward Stance)

‚ñº

Ap Kubi Seogi is a fundamental stance in Taekwondo used for strong forward movements. The front leg is bent at the knee while the back leg is straight. This stance provides stability and power for both offensive and defensive techniques.

Dwi Kubi Seogi (Back Stance)

‚ñº

Dwi Kubi Seogi is a defensive stance in Taekwondo. The weight is primarily on the back leg, having a 70/30 weight distribution. This stance allows for quick retreats and effective counterattacks.

Juchum Seogi (Sitting Stance)

‚ñº

Juchum Seogi is a low, wide stance that resembles sitting. The feet are positioned wider than shoulder-width apart, with knees bent deeply. This stance provides a strong base for executing powerful techniques and maintaining balance.

Kicks (Ï∞®Í∏∞)

Ap Chagi (Front Kick)

‚ñº

Ap Chagi is Taekwondo's foundational front kick. The practitioner lifts the knee straight up and extends the leg forward to strike with the ball of the foot. This kick is useful to control distance and one of the most basic yet effective techniques in Taekwondo.

Dolly Chagi (Roundhouse Kick)

‚ñº

Dolly Chagi is one of the most powerful and iconic kicks in Taekwondo. The practitioner pivots on the supporting foot while swinging the kicking leg in a circular motion to strike the target with the instep or ball of the foot. This kick is known for its speed and impact, making it a staple in both forms and sparring.

Yop Chagi (Side Kick)

‚ñº

Yop Chagi is one of the most powerful kicks in Taekwondo. The practitioner lifts the knee to the side, extends the leg, and strikes with the heel or edge of the foot. This kick is effective for both offensive and defensive maneuvers, delivering significant force to the target.

Naeryo Chagi (Axe Kick)

‚ñº

Naeryo Chagi is a powerful downward kick resembling the motion of an axe. The practitioner raises the leg high and brings it down forcefully, striking with the heel or sole of the foot. This kick is effective for targeting an opponent's head or shoulders, delivering a strong impact.

Dwi Chagi (Back Kick)

‚ñº

Dwi Chagi is a powerful kick executed by turning the body away from the target and extending the leg backward. The practitioner strikes with the heel, delivering a strong blow to the opponent. This kick is effective for surprise counterattacks and maintaining distance.
